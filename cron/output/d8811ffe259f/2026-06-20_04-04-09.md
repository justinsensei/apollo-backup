💡 Morning Thought — [[Notes/On ADHD productivity apps 20260601164244|On ADHD productivity apps]] (Concepts)

Project: [[B2C expansion 2026 20260601164142]]

- Tiimo is a lightly ADHD-flavored digital planner.
- Saner.ai is a lightly ADHD-flavored personal knowledge management system.

I'm not seeing tools that re-think the experience from the ground up with an agent-first approach. Some opportunities being missed:

- Super-quick thought capture (ideas, notes, to-dos)
- No-text or low-text interface
	- Reduces distraction, feature overwhelm, screen fatigue
	- Also good for dyslexia, the more you can do with less text
	- Maybe even good for users with vision issues?
- Zero-maintenance filing, organizing, synthesizing
- **Context Restoration ("Session Save"):** Restoring complete active working context to combat high (50%+) tab-switching cognitive costs and tab-hoarding.
- **Visual Object Permanence:** Ambient visual cues (not dense text) that keep tasks in active memory without causing cognitive avoidance.
- **Regulation-Aware Triage:** Interfaces that adapt to emotional states, energy depletion, and medication cycles, dynamically shrinking the user's choice space.
- **Resilient re-entry over consistency:** Zero shame-inducing reminders or backlogs upon return; rewarding return rather than punishing absence.
